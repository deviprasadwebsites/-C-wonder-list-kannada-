Explore India with us

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Karnataka. It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century. The ruins of temples, palaces, and market streets attract history lovers. Virupaksha Temple is one of the oldest and most famous temples here. The stone chariot at Vittala Temple is an iconic symbol of Karnataka’s heritage. Visitors also enjoy the scenic beauty of the Tungabhadra River. Hampi is a paradise for photographers and adventure seekers. It remains a must-visit place for those interested in history and architecture.

About us

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a beautiful hill station in Karnataka. It is famous for its coffee plantations and lush green landscapes. The misty hills and cool climate make it a perfect getaway. Abbey Falls is a popular tourist attraction in the region. Raja’s Seat offers a breathtaking view of the sunrise and sunset. The Dubare Elephant Camp allows visitors to interact with elephants. Coorg is also home to the famous Tibetan settlement at Bylakuppe. The region is well known for its delicious cuisine and warm hospitality.

Kannada, a state rich in culture and heritage, is home to several beautiful places that showcase its historical and natural splendor. One such place is Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning Vijayanagara-era temples, ruins, and boulder-strewn landscapes. The city, once a thriving capital, now stands as a testament to the grandeur of ancient Indian architecture. Apart from Hampi, Karnataka boasts breathtaking hill stations like Coorg, which is famous for its lush coffee plantations and serene environment. The state is also home to vibrant cities like Bengaluru, the "Silicon Valley of India, " known for its modern infrastructure and bustling tech industry. Whether it's the pristine beaches of Gokarna, the wildlife of Bandipur National Park, or the intricate carvings of Belur and Halebidu, Kannada-speaking Karnataka offers a diverse and unforgettable experience for every traveler.
